**What happened**:
We upgraded Kueue from 0.15.3 to 0.16.5 version. There were roughly ~900 clusterQueues and ~8.5K workloads on the cluster.
There was an immediate dip of about 2K workloads (metric: `kueue_admitted_active_workloads`) at the time of upgrade, and a corresponding rise in pending workloads. In the next hour the workloads got admitted to previous levels, but this certainly caused a churn.

Looking at the Kueue Controller logs:

We had a few hundred errors related to `localQueue doesn't exist or inactive` which only happened at the time of upgrade:

IIRC, at the beginning of the Kueue project, we expected a small number of ClusterQueues, like dozens of pieces and we haven't optimized Kueue performance based on the number clusterQueues (Our performance tests use only 30 ClusterQueus at maximum).

But, it seems that you deployed 900 ClusterQueues to a cluster, which is the primary slowing-down cause, I guess.
@mimowo We might need to introduce the number of ClusterQueue-oriented performance tests and optimize Kueue performance.
