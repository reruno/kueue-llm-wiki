**What happened**:

In the HA setup, we observed that pods remained `SchedulingGated` and workloads were pending after a leader failover. The pod had warning events indicating `Workload has overlapping preemption targets with another workload`. Also, kueue logs show a lot `workloads.kueue.x-k8s.io "<name>" not found` errors.

As additional context, we also observed that the gap between leader and follower metrics became much larger after the upgrade to `v0.17.0`, which might suggest cache divergence across replicas.

**What you expected to happen**:

After a leader failover, the new leader works properly.

**How to reproduce it (as minimally and precisely as possible)**:

We do not yet have a minimal standalone reproducer, but the problem appears to require all of the following:

1. Run Kueue v0.17.0 in HA mode with at least 2 controller-manager replicas.
3. Let the follower observe workload updates while it is not the active leader.
4. Delete workloads that may still exist in the follower-side scheduler / queue caches.
5. Force a leader failover (in our case, the leader was OOM-killed).
6. Let the former follower become leader and start scheduling.

After failover, the new leader may keep stale preemption targets for deleted workloads and start emitting:

- `workloads.kueue.x-k8s.io "<name>" not found`
- `Workload has overlapping preemption targets with another workload`

**Anything else we need to know?**:

We believe this may be a regression related to the workload delete handling refactor, specifically because:

- workload cache cleanup on delete appears to have moved to the `Reconcile` path on `Get(...)=NotFound`
- however, on follower replicas, `leaderAwareReconciler` returns early on `NotFound` and does not delegate to the underlying reconciler
- this seems to leave stale queue/scheduler cache state on followers
- when such a follower later becomes leader, it schedules using stale workload state
