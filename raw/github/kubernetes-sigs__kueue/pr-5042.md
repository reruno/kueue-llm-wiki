#### What this PR does / why we need it:

This PR is about introducing an experimental AdmissionCheckController that checks the available capacity of nodes before admitting a Workload.

Motivation:

Kueue admits workloads by checking their resource requests against the available capacity of a ClusterQueue. However, Kueue's view of the available resources of a ClusterQueue is not always an accurate depiction of the true available resources of nodes in the cluster - see KEP for an example. This leads to scenarios where Kueue falsely admits a workload only for its pods to stay pending due to lack of resources. 

Additionally, the resource requests of falsely admitted pods count against the capacity of a ClusterQueue causing loss of opportunity for future workloads which could have been scheduled had we not had the aforementioned false admissions.

### Comment by [@VassilisVassiliadis](https://github.com/VassilisVassiliadis) — 2025-04-18T09:17:48Z

Hi @mimowo thanks for the quick reply!

I agree that TAS could use ProvReq and `check-capacity.autoscaling.x-k8s.io` to decide whether a task could fit on the nodes of the cluster. Therefore, the TAS ACC in v0.12 would effectively be a superset of the proposed ACC.

My understanding is that `check-capacity.autoscaling.x-k8s.io` is supported since AutoScaler v1.30, although it was also supported in AutoScaler v1.29 as `check-capacity.kubernetes.io`. I think this means that in v0.12 of kueue we wouldn't be able to use TAS on clusters that are running kubernetes < 1.30. For example, this is problematic for OpenShift as version 4.16 is based on Kubernetes 1.29 and is supported untill Q2 2026.

The benefit of the proposed ACC is that it's very simple as it does just 1 thing: checking whether there's enough capacity on the nodes to support the execution of a workload's pods. This enables it to avoid relying on potentially unavailable features.

So then the gaps the it's aiming to address are 2:

* it is self contained and therefore expected to work on servers which cannot use the latest versions of AutoScaler/Kubernetes
* through its simplicity it can help new developers understand the machinery of Kueue
