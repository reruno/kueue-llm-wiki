  @sohankunkerkar do you have any thoughts on how to achieve that?
  
  I have used the cert-manager pluging with kubectl to manually verify that https://cert-manager.io/v1.0-docs/usage/kubectl-plugin/
  
  1. Fetch initial secret: `kubectl get secret kueue-metrics-server-cert -n kueue-system     -o jsonpath='{.data.tls\.crt}' | base64 -d | openssl x509 -noout -serial`
  2. `kubectl port-forward -n kueue-system svc/kueue-controller-manager-metrics-service 8443:8443 &`
  3. Check that cert output is the same as step one when trying to reach server: `echo | openssl s_client -connect localhost:8443 2>/dev/null | openssl x509 -noout -serial`
  4. Renew certificate: `kubectl cert-manager renew kueue-metrics-cert -n kueue-system`
  5. Do step 1 until 3 again and check that the new outputs from 1 and 3 match.
