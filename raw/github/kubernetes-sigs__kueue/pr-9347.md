### Comment by [@mszadkow](https://github.com/mszadkow) — 2026-03-05T12:37:26Z

I have tested a hierarchical cohort setup where `team-a` and `team-b` sit under the same parent.
I set a Cohort-level BorrowingLimit on `team-a` to see if it caps what `team-a` cqs can borrow across teams.

 It works when `team-a` workloads stop admitting at that exact limit even though `team-b` still has spare quota, and then more workloads get admitted right after I raise `team-a`’s Cohort BorrowingLimit.

Conclusion:

Cohort defined borrowing limits only caps cross-cohort borrowing and has nothing to do with  caping borrowing limit in the cohort (as I initially assumed).
That means the borrowing limit for the cohort metric must have updated description:

`Reports the maximum amount of resources that the Cohort subtree (its child Cohorts and ClusterQueues) can borrow from its parent subtree`
